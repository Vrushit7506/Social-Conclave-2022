Super Core Conclave 2022

Affordable & Clean Energy

By 2030, everyone should have access to cheap and dependable energy services, according to Goal 7 of the Sustainable Development Goals. India is the third-largest energy consumer in the world. Almost all forms of electricity generation generate some waste, hence switching to renewable energy sources is essential. Renewable energy sources not only help to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, but they also provide significant socioeconomic advantages over carbon-based fossil fuels. Renewable energy sources are the answer to India's rising energy demands.

Rural Healthcare

Healthcare is a human right, but in India, 60 percent of the population lacks access to it due to a lack of quality infrastructure, a paucity of skilled medical personnel, and inaccessibility to vital facilities. For low-income rural residents, private medical facilities are unaffordable due to their high cost and often inconsistent quality. In our country, private providers account for 92 percent of healthcare visits, with the urban population accounting for 70 percent. The pandemic has placed enormous demands on public healthcare, necessitating immediate attention to the resolution of the system's profound fault lines and vulnerabilities. Our country is in dire need of an improved infrastructure that ensures affordable, quality and timely healthcare for the rural population.

Sustainability Of Consumer Goods

By 2070, India's economy aspires to be carbon neutral. Consumers must switch to sustainable goods in order to reach this ambitious aim. Sustainable purchasing reduces waste, conserves natural resources, and promotes the growth of the green economy, all of which contribute to the country's environmental objectives. India's emissions account for about 7% of total global emissions. While the movement has gained traction globally, with brands adopting more sustainable practices, ethical sourcing, and lowering their carbon footprint, consumer concerns in India are still minor, but they are beginning to affect brands. Encourage industries, enterprises, and consumers to recycle and minimize waste to help developing countries shift to more sustainable consumption patterns.
